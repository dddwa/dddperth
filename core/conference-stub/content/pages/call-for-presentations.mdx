The CFP for DevConf Example is open between **1 June** and **15 July** each year.
The submission link goes live in early June at our Sessionize page.

## We want to hear from you

DevConf Example especially welcomes submissions from:

- First-time speakers
- Underrepresented groups in tech
- Practitioners sharing real-world experience (not just polished case studies)

## What we're looking for

- **Lightning talks** — 5 to 10 minutes, focused on one idea
- **Standard talks** — 30 to 45 minutes
- **Workshops** — 90 minutes to half a day (smaller capacity)

Topics: anything software-industry related. Tech deep-dives, soft skills, career
stories, war stories, weird and wonderful side projects — all welcome.

## How submissions are selected

Submissions go through community voting once the CFP closes:

1. **CFP open** — anyone can submit, multiple talks allowed
2. **Anonymous attendee voting** — registered attendees rate submissions blind (author names hidden)
3. **Programme committee review** — final selection balances voting results, topic diversity, and speaker mix

This means the agenda is shaped by who's actually attending — not by who has the
most followers.

## Speaker support

Selected speakers receive:

- A free conference ticket (obviously)
- Travel reimbursement up to a fixed cap (varies by speaker location)
- Speaker dinner the evening before
- Slide review and rehearsal slots offered by the programme committee
- Mentorship from past speakers if requested (especially for first-timers)

## Submit

Submissions go through Sessionize. The link appears on the home page once the CFP
window opens.
