## When and where?

DevConf Example 2025 runs on **18 October 2025** at the Example Convention Centre
in San Francisco. (Reminder: this is a fictional conference. Don't book flights.)

## How much do tickets cost?

Tickets are pay-what-you-can in this fictional universe, but the rendered ticket
release stages show **$50 Early Bird**, **$80 General**, and **$120 Last Minute**
to exercise the multi-release rendering in core.

## Who can attend?

Anyone, in our fictional universe. The real conferences that use this codebase
generally aim to be inclusive and welcoming to all participants regardless of
experience level, background, or role.

## Will lunch be provided?

Yes — three rotating zones with vegetarian, meat, and gluten-free options. The stub
demonstrates the food-info rendering with three example meals.

## Can I sponsor?

Sponsorship is what makes community conferences possible. See the [sponsorship
page](/sponsorship) for tier descriptions. To enquire, email
[sponsorship@example.test](mailto:sponsorship@example.test) — though as a fictional
address, your email will not be answered.

## What's the dress code?

Whatever you're comfortable in. Most attendees go for casual or business-casual.

## Will talks be recorded?

In the fictional canon of DevConf Example: yes, all main-stage talks are recorded
and posted to YouTube within four weeks of the conference. Forks should update this
section with their real recording policy.

## Where can I leave feedback?

After the conference, a feedback form opens at `/feedback` and stays open for one
week. The link only appears once the conference date has passed.
